CS Professors & Lecturers

Overview

Every quarter, you are assigned two pass times - Pass 1 and Pass 2. Unfortunately, picking classes is often a troubling and difficult task as we college students suffer from decision fatigue, incomplete ratemyprofessor listings, course conflicts, and the neverending waitlist. This page serves to help you make superior decisions and better utilize your time and schedule with your long-term agenda. For each class, there is a subject matter breakdown, relevant fields in Computer Science, importance in regard to employability, professor recommendations, and general advice.

Sean Davis

Look to your left. Look to your right. One of you three will not be here in two weeks.Sean Davis

Summary

Sean Davis has been a lecturer at UC Davis for many years, and is known as the Gatekeeper of Computer Science, since he teaches most of the ECS 40 classes and all of ECS 60. As such, everyone must get past him in order to get to upperdivs (unless you take the equivalent courses at Community College). Unfortunately, he is retiring at the end of 2017. He asks that everyone call him Sean, Mr. Davis, or Master Davis (preferably Sean). He is known for having comparatively rigorous courses, but has an extremely solid curriculum and knows his subject matter extremely well. I recommend for everyone to take his class, or you will find it EXTREMELY difficult to succeed in interviews/the real world. He teaches 5 classes: ECS 30, 40, 50, 60, and 154A. 40 and 60 are his bread and butter classes, though his ECS 30 is extremely well taught as well. When you talk to Sean, don't just talk and ask for help - think about what he's saying. Then he'll be more willing to help you, since you aren't just searching for answers.
Overall, Sean is extremely helpful and understanding, and a generally cheerful/friendly person. Take the time to get to know him; it'll help alot.

ECS 30: Although Sean has not taught this course for several years, people who do well in this course typically do extremely well in 40 and 60. If you have the opportunity, always take Sean's 30.

ECS 40: I highly recommend taking Sean's ECS 40 class, or you will struggle immensely in ECS 60. This class is rather time consuming, and has extensive use of pointers and memory management. Be sure to fully understand the prompts before you begin.

ECS 50: He is known for being easier for ECS 50 than the other lecturer - Butner. This is because he teaches MIPS and CUSP, as opposed to MIPS and Intel x86 Assembly. CUSP stands for Carleton's Utterly Simple preprocessor, and is a ficticious language created to simplify assembly. It is not used anywhere in the real world.

ECS 60: Sean is the only teacher for ECS 60. His first 3 programs are basically free points, with the last 2 finally requiring implementation and application of course material to actual problems. His tests are straightforward and fair, especially since he gives practice tests beforehand.

ECS 154A: Sean is also known for being easier than Butner for this class as well.

Out of classes, Sean was a house painter for many years and often travels to Europe to watch theater.

Norm Matloff

Summary

Matloff has an interesting teaching style that some people love, but others hate. Personally, I think its great. All of the lecture materials are in the textbook, and he is able to simply talk through the material and write any further explanations on the board, while you follow along NOT TAKING NOTES since the notes are all in the textbook already. Also, rather than having a few high-pressure events (midterms), he splits up the grade into weekly quizzes such that you are less able to procrastinate.

Karl Levitt

Summary

Karl Levitt is the second oldest professor on campus (the oldest is a Chemistry professor). He is a nice man who loves teaching, but isn't really strict or hard in his teaching, resulting in easy classes but not much material learned. For ECS 160, he uses Android (as opposed to iOS). If you want an easy A, take his class. If you want to learn a ton but do a ton of work, take Nitta.

Chris Nitta

Summary

Nitta is known for being the extremely difficult professor for ECS 150, which is his bread and butter course. He is rather difficult in all of the classes he teaches, but you become extremely well acquainted with the material.
